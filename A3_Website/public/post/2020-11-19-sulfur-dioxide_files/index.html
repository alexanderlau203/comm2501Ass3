Sulfur Dioxide

Sulfur dioxide (SO2) can irritate skin, eyes, nose throat and lungs. In more severe cases, irritation of the respiratory system can occur, causing breathing difficulties.

SO2 Across the Nation

SO2 levels in the US are also very restrained, with 95% of the participating states below an AQI of 10 and the majority clustered below 5.

The distribution of SO2 across the country doesn’t seem to yield a particular geographical pattern, only showing concentrations in Alaska and Ohio.

The distribution of concentrations is also fairly healthy. Even in the highest producing SO2 states, there are only a few days in the year where the SO2 AQI levels reach over 50. The only minor concern being Indiana, with quite a few high AQI outliers.

SO2 By Source

In the graph below, the greatest source of SO2 production comes from stationary fuel combustion. However, from 2006-2015, this has drastically improved, decreasing to almost a third of its original emissions output. The remaining sources have remained the fairly stable and low over this period.
