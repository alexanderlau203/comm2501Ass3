O3 Across the Nation

Ozone is the only air pollutant that has not improved between 2000 and 2015. This is reflected in our most recent data set, which shows the average ozone AQI level reaching over 40 in 8 states.

Similar to NO2, the ozone seems to be concentrated on near Utah and the surrounding states, as well as the major East Coast cities. Tenesee, however, seems to be the exception to this trend.

Examining the four worst states also shows alarmingly high AQI peaks.

While the majority of readings stay under 50, peaks in Utah have surpassed approach the ‘Unhealthy’ category, in which some members of the general public may experience health effects.

O3 By Source

The data set does not include O3 by source as O3 is not directly produced by humans. Rather, nitrous oxides and volatile organic compounds produced by humans react together to create O3.
