Overall, air pollutants have been declining from the 2000s as due to better regulations, economic shifts and other technological innovations. However, air quality constantly fluctuates and can pose health risks to people who are sensitive to air pollution, such as the elderly.

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a measure of air quality. An AQI over 50 is considered to moderate health threat, however, the EPA has aimed for even lower targets, which are specified in the NAAQS Table. Below, four major pollutant trends have been summarised for the US as a whole:

The declines for NO2, SO2 and CO AQIs are promising, declining by approximately 15, 10 and 5 points respectively. However, ground-level ozone has largely remained the same around 38.
