NO2 Across the Nation

In the most recent data set (2015), Colorado averaged the worst AQI at 36. It was also followed by like New York and Utah both at approximately 29.8.

Geographically, there appears to be two major concentrations of NO2. The first being near just East of Central America involving Colarado, Utah and Arizona. The second is locaetd on the East cost, involving more population dense areas like New York and New Jersey. In the interactive chart below, click on the circles for the exact numbers for NO2 levels and AQI.

Looking at the 4 worst states, the distribution of their daily NO2 AQI levels show some worrying results.

In Colorado, most of the AQI measurements are concentrated around 45-50, which puts that state at a ‘moderate’ health risk. In addition, Colorado, New Jersey and New York all show significant peaks on certain days, which come close to the ‘Unhealthy for sensitive groups’ category (AQI of 100).
